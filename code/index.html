Must travel

Phuket

Jade-hued waves concealing rainbows of fish wash white-gold beaches wrapped in Phuketian heritage: Phuket (ภูเก็ต), Thailand's dazzling largest island, is so diverse you may never want to leave.

Bangkok

Same same, but different. This Thailish T-shirt philosophy sums up Bangkok, a city where the familiar and the exotic collide like the flavors on a plate of pàt tai.

Chiang mai

The former seat of the Lanna kingdom is a blissfully calm place to relax and recharge your batteries – in a city still firmly Thai in its atmosphere and attitude.

Pattaya

Pattaya is well-known as the party capital of Thailand. Owing to its nightlife, the city draws many tourists from around the world. Pattaya can be termed as a perfect amalgam of modern with an edge of rustic.

Thai Festivals Timeline

April

Songkran Festival

Celebrated in April, Songkran is the Thai New Year's festival known for its water fights and vibrant celebrations.

November

Loi Krathong Festival

Loi Krathong, usually in November, involves floating decorative baskets on water to pay respects to the river spirits.

June/July

Phi Ta Khon Festival

Taking place in June or July, Phi Ta Khon involves colorful masks, lively parades, and traditional dances in Loei Province.

May

Royal Ploughing Ceremony

The Royal Ploughing Ceremony in May marks the beginning of the rice-growing season, with symbolic rituals performed by the royal family.
